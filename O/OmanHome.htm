The desert rocks, the ocean rolls
in the sunny Sultanate of Oman !

You've gotta love a country where the cops write you a tougher ticket for driving a dusty car than for speeding happily down the highway.

You've gotta love a country where the national cuisine is a palate-perking blend of Lebanese, Greek, Indian, Pakistani & Arabian dishes. Where children & seniors are revered, & people really do rest on their day of rest. Where education & medical care are free for most citizens. Where robberies, rapes, assaults & chemical abuse are virtually nonexistent. Where the weather is sunny & only moderately humid most of the year. Where nobody goes homeless. Not even the Bedouin nomads who, straddling their sleepy camels, drift the seas of Wahiba Sands like meditative gypsies on Hejira.

OK guys, time to scrap your neckties. Gals, kick off your shoes. Get set for some big fun on the Arabian Peninsula:

  • Scuba diving in Oman.     Oman's diving islands offer bustling fish schools & big critter encounters that invoke the underwater zest of Costa Rica's Pacific coast or Mexico's Sea of Cortez. (Here's a plus -- the Oman Dive Center, just 25 minutes from downtown Muscat, is one of the most luxuriously appointed scuba operations we have reviewed anywhere in the world.) This impressive multi-million-dollar complex spans a pristine white sand cove, is crisply managed, safety conscious & friendly. Please check our extensive article on Arabian Sea diving in the Gulf of Oman. But there's so much more to see here in the modern Arabian world -- the safe sector of Olde Arabia.

  • Desert camping.     This is surreal serene adventure, with fragrant air & towering dunes surrounding you. Some of these dunes are beautiful as a mind massage -- waves of pastel colors from dusty rose to cat's-eye yellow & doeskin brown -- shape shifters that never sleep. Way back in the Wahiba Sands, some dunes are steep enough to snowboard down. The Wahiba Sands are one of the world's most scenic desert regions; & we recommend you visit soon.

  • Caving in Oman.     The world's second-largest cave chamber is begging for a deeper peek -- after a mere 500-foot free rappel to the bottom -- while many smaller (safer!) & richly ornamented caves perforate the desert mountains of Oman, ripe for willing spelunkers.

  • Hundreds of huge forts.     Built centuries ago, these mystery-rich Omani forts loom up from the desert sands all across this country. Many of their original cannons, muskets, brass artifacts & secret passageways are still preserved, just as they were in the glory days when gunpowder was high-tech & dried dates were "power bars." Even if you are leery of historical tours as I used to be, these forts are fascinating. Many Omani forts are located only a couple easy driving hours from Muscat. They are fabulous fun!

  • Canyon trekking in Oman.     The remote mountain gorges that drain into river valleys are called "wadis" in Oman. Wadis are the arteries of life in the Arabian world. There's a wild-hare sport the local expats call "wadi bashing," which I have renamed "SWEKKING." TM   This means swim-trekking down virgin desert canyons, shooting small rapids on your back (no raft baby!), soaked to the skin & racing to beat the rains. Why the need to rush? Rainfalls are a rare but rampant force that can trigger flash floods. Down in the flatlands & wadi deltas, rain in the mountains means new green life for weeks; so the villagers dance & rejoice. But back in the steep scenic canyonlands, rain can mean Goodnight Irene. This glorious reckless sport grabs you by the collar & pulls you deep down, right next to the pulsing heart muscle of the Arabian desert. And you go, breathless & willing, utterly happy to round the next riverbend & see what's happening. After your wadi trek you can drive down & camp overnight in the Wahiba Sands for a grande finale.

SWEKKING TM   may be the ultimate desert cardio-sport. It's a mix of cliff scrambling, ledge plunging, boulder hopping, gravel hiking & rapids running. Swekking is a soggy-boot mess that'll have you laughing like a banshee as you stride 300 level meters, then swim 300 more, then clamber over cabana-sized boulders, then swim with your pack some more, repeating that rocky mantra for hours on end, always staggering to shore with a backpack spewing 40 lbs of green river water, & canyon walls overhead so colorful they would start the heart of any jaded National Geographic editor. But laughing with your soaked-to-the-bone friends -- laughing & puffing with honest exercise -- that's the best part about swekking.

Yes, I am hopelessly enthralled by these wadis of Oman. Did I mention, there are scores of easier wadis, with gentle rock scrambles & low-angle hikes for the middle-agers & shutterbugs. There's a wadi out there with your name on it, manic or mellow, whatever you need. So whatever you do, watch out for this swekking bug. It's more catchy than you'd imagine from an air conditioned NorthAm armchair.

NO MADISON AVENUE MARKETING:
Reality check, funseekers. This place called Oman is not the Madison Avenue fantasy of ad-blotto dive magazines. No hot-air marketing is necessary. This country will sell itself in time. Oman is a nation of people who are honest, responsible & sincere in their hospitality. Oman is a nation so wealthy in natural resources -- clean air, clean water, clean desert sands, & a clean value system -- that I feel embarrassed for never knowing about it before. It's not a perfect country, of course, as such things exist only in fiction. There is no classic democracy here, rather a landed aristocracy. Nobody can criticize the Sultan's policies & all telecommunications from phones to TV to e-mails are firmly under government control. But folks, this is a society that works. It works in every sense. So who are we to judge a social order this decent & easygoing? The cultural chauvinists who currently run many sectors of our North American & European schools have a lot to learn from the Islamic people of Oman. Arabian Sea diving is about more than just chewing a regulator & marveling at fish life. The people here may be the best part of the trip.

TIME to QUASH OLD PREJUDICES:
All kidding aside, here's the rub. If Moses himself couldn't do it all the way, who's going to reshape the parched clay of western thinking overnight? One sun-baked Yankee diver prattling on about faraway Oman? Unlikely, but let's try to make a little dent here. There are centuries of knuckleheaded prejudice to overcome, even among open-minded adventurers in Europe & North America. So many North Americans are wary of the Arab World, just as I was, & this article aims to set the facts straight. Relax dive buddies, breathe deeply & take the plunge with your eyes wide open. Oman is a place you need to visit. Let me say this again: if you have visited the Jaded Guru -- Bendar Dundat -- if you think you've truly seen it all, then Oman is a must-see for you.

Air tickets are super-cheap from Europe. Flights from Amsterdam, London, Athens, Frankfurt, are duck soup, one quick day & no fuss. For North Americans & clients from Latino America, air prices & travel times are equivalent to any South Pacific sojourn, nothing intimidating. You need just one overnight in Amsterdam or London on the way over. Sure, this is not a pratfall-easy trip. If Oman was an easy dive holiday, a predictable-as-a-cheeseburger option like Roatan or Cozumel, it would long ago have been stampeded by party-hearty college kids on their annual Spring Break hormone crusades. The flights to Oman are nowhere near a grind, & the rewards are bound to rock your soul.

Since returning from my first diving holiday in Oman -- more than 12 years ago -- one key question dogs me almost daily. What is wrong with our educational system, & with our national doors of perception, that the western world has virtually ignored a country this hospitable & visually appealing -- for centuries? This is dive travel, better yet, adventure travel right out of Robert Frost's "road not taken."

HOW DO YOU GET to OMAN?
Where in the world is Oman, you ask? The Sultanate of Oman is a country shaped like a mirror image of California, but a little bit smaller. Oman is nestled right at the "bottom right hand corner" of the vast Arabian Peninsula, a safe & sound 1500 miles from the mess in Israel & Lebanon. Oman is just southeast of our friends in Saudi Arabia, south of the legendary Rub al Khali desert, & also due east of the laid-back United Arab Emirates. Oman's classic Arabian capital, Muscat, is home for a bit more than 1 million Muslim souls. Muscat for centuries has been the multi-cultural gem of the Arabian Sea. Some European travel pros have called Oman the "Switzerland of the Arab World." Time we took a deeper look into Oman vacations, donï¿½t you think?

WHAT ARE YOU THINKING, THEY WONDERED?
At first mention, close friends & family assured me I was nuts to even think of visiting the Arabian region that month. November 2000 was flashpoint for one of the worst "intifadah" outbreaks in Palestine since the Six-Day War. Most of the Arab world was infuriated by Israeli occupation of the West Bank. Tensions were high across the Middle East. Furthermore, I went to Oman just a few days after the USS Cole incident in Aden, Yemen. This seemed not exactly prime time for some lanky Yankee to be tooling around the Arabian Peninsula in search of outdoor bliss.

A father of two little boys, & senior partner in a fast-growing firm, I have a pretty conservative strategy when it comes to choosing new destinations. As Woody Allen says, "I have this low threshold of death." No more 20-something bravado in these 50-something bones. I went to the Arabian world expecting at best a cold shoulder everywhere, & at worst -- visions of Molotovs dancing in my head.

Oman alive was I wrong. Big time wrong!

After 30 years of high-voltage adventure travel on five continents & across the South Pacific, I'll admit that I blew it by pre-judging the Arabian Peninsula to be risky for North American & European travelers. Oman erased four decades of misconceptions that western culture has engrained into urban white guys my age. Oman blessed my life with a geographic & spiritual journey never to be forgotten. Take a few minutes to walk with me through this truly wonderful land.

Oman has strict laws against merchants fleecing customers, so you can find some of the best bargains on earth for gold & silver artifacts -- all wonderfully high-test alloys -- plus my favorite gift of frankincense, a pure sweet-smelling resin from the coastal Boswellia trees. Souvenir hunters will be astonished at the good deals in Muscat's Souq. It's big fun to round off any Oman dive vacations with a gift-hunting foray over to this Souq. This market will blow your mind but not your budget.

IMPORTANT NOTE about PRICES & TARIFFS:
 First things first for your Oman scuba diving holiday. Please remember that you will need to secure a formal visa from the Omani Consulate closest to your hometown, before you will be allowed entry into Oman. Visas for most U.S., Canadian & European citizens are very easy to secure, & take only a couple weeks. The Oman Embassy staffers in Washington, DC are exceptionally gracious & helpful. They are geared up to encourage eco-responsible tourism; this is quite evident. Visa fees are nominal & paid via cashier's check to the Omani Embassy or Consul General nearest to you. Make sure you prepare well for your Oman scuba diving trip.

OMAN VISA PROCESS -- Passport & visa required for all tourists, & for all Oman dive vacations. Tourist/business visas for multiple-entry issued for stays up to 6 months & valid for 2 years. Both business or tourist visas require a special official application form & cholera immunization if arriving from infected area & a self-addressed stamped envelope for return of passport by mail. To clarify any details regarding your Omani visa, please check with the Embassy of the Sultanate of Oman to see what rules apply -- 2535 Belmont Rd., N.W., Washington, D.C. 20008 (202/387-1980, -1981 or -1982).
