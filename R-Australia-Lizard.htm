Ensconced on a private atoll with a lagoon so magical in hue -- a meld of aquamarine, turquoise, emerald green & electric royal blue -- Lizard Island is 27 kilometers across the Coral Sea from the nearest car, TV, newspaper or traffic-riddled city. The island has been designated an Australian National Park -- it's truly that special. Take a peek at the guest register & you'll see signatures of royalty, politicians & actors of renown.

After scuba diving or snorkeling around a lagoon that offers a myriad perfect bommies with fish & coral in wild abundance -- try the Giant Clam Gardens where the denizens are nearly 150 years old! -- you may want to unwind even further with archery, hikes to a scenic outlook 1,100 feet above the ocean, birdwatching, catamaran sailing, wind surfing, water skiing, glass-bottom paddle-boats, chef-catered picnics or tennis.

The reefs here are only a few mellow yards from your beach, & so easily accessible it will amaze you. It is no effort to just step into the ocean & in seconds you're in the heart of the Outer Great Barrier Reef. It's all right there in your mask, so close you'll be hypnotized by the gentle beauty & rich colors. The diversity of fish & coral life is dazzling, even on those few gray-weather days. You'll be tempted to dive again & again every day. Australia diving vacations get under your skin, in a wonderful way. Few dive resorts in the world can match Lizard Island.

For scuba enthusiasts, the world-famous Cod Hole site where giant potato cods in the 200-300-lb range are thriving, is only a short boat trip from Lizard Island. Nearly every week of the year, scuba diving yachts from Cairns (150 miles to the South) zoom up to Lizard Island to experience the adventure of Cod Hole. It's right in your own back yard! How do we put this, not too cheekily? Cod Hole is to Australia's scuba diving scene as the Statue of Liberty is to New York City.

Now there's another way to play in the perfect waters near Cod Hole. Besides being a scuba diving Mecca, & a haven for Australia snorkeling enthusiasts, Lizard Island draws game fishermen like an azure magnet. The clear, clean waters surrounding Lizard Island are regarded as one of the world's best fishing grounds for big game species, especially huge prizes such as the elusive black marlin. Prime time for the big ones is August through December. Sport fishermen from all over the world come to Lizard Island to ply their skill & strength against these giants, some of which have weighed in at more than 1,000 lbs!

There's a Royal Flying Doctor clinic right on the premises, which maintains ready radio contact with mainland medical air-evacuation teams in the unlikely event of an emergency.

Australia snorkeling packages always include the best part free -- the snorkeling of course! Lizard Island even has its own small bank & library. Major credit cards are accepted as well. HISTORICAL BACKGROUND of LIZARD ISLAND:
During his celebrated exploratory voyage of 1770, the legendary Captain James Cook climbed the high hill on Lizard Island to look out over the Great Barrier Reef nearby, & from there he bagan to chart a course out to sea through the labyrinth of tricky reefs awaiting his vessels. Today Lizard Island offers an escape of a different kind. A unique holiday on the Great Barrier Reef's northern most island resort.

If you're looking for something out of the ordinary, then Lizard Island is your cup of tea. This is by any standard of international measurement, one of Australia's best 5-star luxury resorts. Being right on the Great Barrier Reef is rare enough, but Lizard Island offers the full spectrum - both the inner & outer Reef experiences.

Here you can snorkel off a secluded beach to discover a garden of giant clams, many a metre long & some reputed to be over 120 years old. & there are 23 more soft, powdery white beaches just waiting to be explored. Lizard Island is renowned for its diving. At the world famous 'Cod Hole', you'll come face to face with a massive yet curious potato cod swimming right up to inspect you with child-like curiosity. When you're scuba diving, you're their buddy.

Whilst the fish at Cod Hole may not respect your privacy, we certainly will. In fact, we'll pack a picnic lunch for two & guide you to a 'private beach' to enjoy this magic location undisturbed. There are many fun & refreshing walks, & on at least one you can expect to glimpse a monitor, the lizard after which the island was named. Healthy appetites -- honed by the Australia snorkeling or Australia scuba diving efforts -- are then rewarded with superb dining - fresh local produce prepared with European & Asian influence. All island meals & most activities are included in the tariff & although remote, ferry access from Gladstone (departing at about 11 am each day) is easy.
